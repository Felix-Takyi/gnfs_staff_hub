Our Heritage

History of the Ghana National Fire Service

1937
Colonial Origins

The Gold Coast Fire Brigade was established during the British colonial era, initially based in Accra to protect government buildings and the merchant district.

1963
Ghana National Fire Service Founded

After independence, the Gold Coast Fire Brigade was reorganised and rebranded as the Ghana National Fire Service under Act 537, placing it under the Ministry of the Interior.

1980s
Nationwide Expansion

GNFS expanded operations across all 10 regions of Ghana, establishing district and municipal fire stations and introducing specialist units including HAZMAT and rescue teams.

2003
Modernisation Drive

Fleet modernisation and international training partnerships with the UK, Germany and the US upgraded GNFS response capabilities to international standards.

Today
21st Century Fire Service

GNFS now operates 22 fire stations across all 16 regions with over 5,000 personnel, embracing digital management tools, community outreach, and continuous training.
